## Round 1

### Dungeon Master (6.6s)

*I adjust my grip on my warhammer and check the straps on my shield one final time. The morning mist clings to the rocky entrance, and I can smell the acrid scent of sulfur drifting from within.*

"Ten years I've waited for this moment," *I mutter, my voice echoing slightly off the cave walls.* "Ironforge Clan... your deaths will be avenged this day."

*I take a deep breath, my weathered face set with grim determination. The reward gold means nothing compared to finally facing one of these cursed wyrms again. I step forward into the darkness, my boots crunching on loose stone.*

"Come then, beast. Let's see if you burn as well as my kinfolk did."

*I raise my shield and advance cautiously into the cave, hammer at the ready, every sense alert for the first sign of the dragon's presence.*

### Thorin (3.4s)

*The darkness within the cave quickly swallows the last vestiges of the morning light, though a faint, pulsating orange glow seems to emanate from deeper within. The air grows heavier, thick with the smell of sulfur and something else... something ancient and reptilian. Your boots crunch on more than just loose stone now; scattered bones, some remarkably large, litter the floor, bleached white and brittle.*

*The passage twists and turns, each step taking you further into the mountain's maw. The faint glow ahead intensifies slightly, casting dancing shadows that seem to writhe and coil like unseen serpents. A low, guttural rumble vibrates through the very rock beneath your feet, a sound that shakes your teeth and resonates deep in your chest. It's not a growl, not yet, but rather the sound of a truly massive creature breathing, stirring.*

*Ahead, the passage opens into a vast cavern. The orange light pulses brighter now, revealing towering stalagmites and stalactites that look like teeth in some colossal maw. And then you see it -- a mountain of glittering gold and precious artifacts, piled high in the center of the cavern. Perched atop this hoard, coiled like a sleeping cat, is the dragon. Its scales shimmer with the colour of molten copper, and even in slumber, it exudes an aura of immense power. Smoke curls lazily from its nostrils with each deep, rumbling breath.*

*You've found your quarry, Thorin. It sleeps, for now, its massive head resting on a pile of dwarven helms, a grim trophy.*

## Round 2

### Dungeon Master (9.0s)

*My breath catches in my throat as I see those familiar helms beneath the beast's head. My knuckles go white around my hammer's grip, and for a moment, rage threatens to overwhelm caution.*

"Ironforge helms..." *I whisper through gritted teeth, recognizing the distinctive craftsmanship even from this distance.*

*I force myself to pause at the cavern's edge, every instinct screaming at me to charge forward and bury my hammer in the creature's skull. But ten years of planning this moment has taught me patience. I study the sleeping dragon, noting the rise and fall of its massive flanks, the way its tail coils around the far side of the hoard.*

*Moving with practiced stealth despite my heavy armor, I begin to circle the chamber's perimeter, staying in the shadows cast by the towering stone formations. My eyes scan for any advantage - loose rocks above, narrow passages for retreat, anything that might give a lone dwarf a fighting chance against such a monster.*

*As I move, I murmur an old dwarven prayer under my breath:* "Moradin, forge-father, guide my hammer true. Let the spirits of my clan witness this day."

*I pause behind a thick stalagmite, weighing my options - wake it with a challenge and face it head-on with honor, or try to strike while it sleeps and gain what advantage I can.*

### Thorin (5.6s)

*Your heavy armor, usually a clanking testament to dwarven craftsmanship, seems to absorb the sound of your movement, a testament to your practiced stealth. The cavern is vast, far larger than you initially perceived, its ceiling lost in the gloom high above...*

*The dragon is indeed coiled atop the highest point of its hoard. Its head rests on the dwarven helms, facing roughly towards the main entrance, but its eyes are closed... The tail, thick as a tree trunk, sweeps around the far side of the hoard, partially obscuring a narrow fissure in the cavern wall -- perhaps a secondary exit.*

*You've made it about a quarter of the way around the perimeter, still well within the shadows, without disturbing the beast. The narrow fissure on the far side of the hoard, partially hidden by the dragon's tail, seems to be the only potentially interesting feature beyond the immediate confrontation.*

*The decision weighs heavily. A direct assault on a sleeping dragon might gain you an initial surprise, but it also denies you the chance to assess its full capabilities in a fair fight. Yet, the sight of your clan's helms gnaws at your resolve, urging you to strike now, with prejudice.*

## Round 3

### Dungeon Master (9.9s)

*I crouch behind the stalagmite, my jaw clenched as I stare at those desecrated helms. The tactical part of my mind wars with the burning need for vengeance. After a long moment, I slowly straighten, decision made.*

*Moving with deliberate care, I step out from behind my cover and into the flickering light of the cavern. My boots ring clearly against the stone as I stride toward the hoard, no longer attempting stealth. When I'm perhaps thirty paces from the sleeping wyrm, I plant my feet wide and raise my warhammer high.*

"DRAGON!" *My voice booms through the cavern, echoing off the walls with the force of ten years' worth of rage and grief.* "I am Thorin Ironforge, last son of Clan Ironforge! You sleep upon the helms of my kinfolk - my father, my brothers, my cousins!"

*I strike my hammer against my shield, the metallic clang sharp and challenging.*

"Rise and face me, coward! Face the dwarf whose family you murdered! I've come to collect a debt written in blood and flame!"

*My eyes never leave the dragon's form as I continue, my voice carrying the formal cadence of an old dwarven challenge:* "By hammer and forge, by stone and steel, I call you to account for your crimes! Wake and die with what honor a wyrm can muster!"

*I shift into a combat stance, shield raised, hammer ready, prepared for the terrible moment when those ancient eyes open.*

### Thorin (6.2s)

Your challenge, amplified by the cavern's acoustics, rips through the heavy air. The low rumble that filled the chamber abruptly ceases, replaced by an unnerving silence that seems to press down on you. For a heart-stopping moment, nothing happens.

Then, a tremor. Not of the ground, but of the immense, coiled form atop the hoard. One massive eye, a slit of molten gold, slowly, deliberately, opens. Then the other. They fix upon you with an ancient, predatory intelligence that chills you to the bone, a gaze that has witnessed centuries of life and death.

A guttural, rasping sound, like rocks grinding together, emanates from the dragon's throat. Its immense head slowly lifts from the pile of dwarven helms... The sound is like thunder, and the air around you grows noticeably warmer, crackling with latent energy.

It lowers its head slightly, bringing its massive snout closer to your level, though still towering over you. Its voice, when it comes, is a deep, resonant rumble that vibrates through the very stone, a sound of immense power and ancient arrogance.

"A *dwarf*," it hisses, the word laced with contempt, "daring to disturb my slumber? And for such a petty reason as... 'kinfolk'?" A low, rumbling chuckle, devoid of humor, shakes its massive chest. "These trinkets? They were merely... *found*. And now they serve their purpose, a comfortable pillow for a king."

## Round 4

### Dungeon Master (9.8s)

*The dragon's words hit me like hammer blows, but I stand firm, my shield steady despite the tremor in my hands. The casual dismissal of my clan as "trinkets" sends white-hot fury coursing through my veins.*

"TRINKETS?!" *I roar, my voice cracking with rage.* "Those were WARRIORS, you overgrown lizard! Each helm represents a life - fathers, sons, brothers who died defending their homes while you burned them alive!"

*I take a step forward, my boots crunching on scattered coins, refusing to be cowed by the creature's immense presence.*

"Found them, did you? FOUND THEM?!" *Spittle flies from my lips as decades of grief pour out.* "You found them after you razed Ironhold to ash! After you melted the great forges my ancestors built over centuries! After you turned children into charcoal and mothers into memory!"

*I raise my hammer higher, the weapon seeming pitifully small against the dragon's bulk, but my voice carries unwavering conviction.*

"I am the last because YOU made me the last! And aye, I've come to join them - but not as your victim, wyrm. I've come to send you to whatever hell spawned you!"

*My eyes blaze with righteous fury as I shift my stance, ready to charge.*

"So choose your death, ancient one - will you face me like the 'king' you claim to be, or cower atop your stolen gold like the grave-robbing worm you are?"

### Thorin (4.9s)

The dragon's golden eyes narrow to furious slits, its contemptuous amusement replaced by a slow, simmering rage. Your words, sharp and defiant, pierce through its ancient arrogance...

"A worm, you say?" The resonant voice drops, becoming a low, dangerous growl... "You speak of honor, little dwarf? You speak of *theft*? I am a force of nature, a living inferno! I take what I desire, and what I take becomes *mine*! The weak perish, and the strong endure. That is the only law that matters!"

With a sudden, explosive movement, the dragon uncoils from its hoard. Gold and gems scatter like a tidal wave as its immense body slams onto the cavern floor... Its vast wings snap open, fully extended, brushing against the distant ceiling and creating a powerful downdraft that whips your beard and cloak around you.

"You wish to join your kin, Thorin Ironforge?" the dragon hisses, its voice now a roaring inferno of sound, vibrating through your bones. "Then *burn*!"

The light in its throat reaches an unbearable intensity, a prelude to a blast of pure, molten fire. The attack is imminent.
