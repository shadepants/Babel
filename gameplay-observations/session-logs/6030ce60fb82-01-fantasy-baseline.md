## Round 1

### Dungeon Master (7.6s)

*I adjust my weathered chainmail and grip my axe handle tighter, the familiar weight bringing some comfort. My jaw clenches as I stare into the dark mouth of the cave.*

"Ten years I've waited for this moment," *I mutter, my voice rough with old pain.* "Ten years since that cursed wyrm turned my home to ash and my kinfolk to bone."

*I run my thumb along the notched blade of my axe - each mark a fallen enemy, but none yet a dragon.*

"The people of Millhaven deserve peace, aye... but this beast owes a blood debt to Clan Ironforge that's long overdue."

*I take a deep breath of the crisp mountain air, knowing it might be my last, and step toward the cave entrance.*

"Come then, dragon. Let's see if your flames burn hotter than a dwarf's vengeance."

*I raise my shield and advance into the darkness, my boots echoing off the stone walls.*

### Thorin (9.0s)

**[Dungeon Master]**: *As you step into the cave, the temperature drops noticeably. Your footsteps echo in the vast chamber ahead, and the scent of sulfur grows stronger with each step. The passage opens into a massive cavern, its ceiling lost in shadow.*

*Suddenly, a melodic voice drifts from the darkness - not the rumbling roar you expected, but something almost... musical.*

**"Another hero comes seeking glory? How tiresome."**

*Golden eyes pierce the gloom as a sleek form emerges from behind a pile of glittering treasure. The dragon is smaller than you imagined - perhaps only thirty feet long - with scales that shimmer between deep emerald and midnight blue.*

**"I am Aria, and I grow weary of this dance. Tell me, little dwarf, do you come here to die like the others, or do you perhaps possess something more interesting than a sharp blade and wounded pride?"**

*She settles onto her haunches, tail curling around her like a cat, but her eyes never leave you. Despite her casual tone, you can see the deadly intelligence there.*

**"Speak quickly. My patience wears thin, and the morning grows late."**

*The cavern falls silent except for the soft sound of your breathing and the distant drip of water somewhere in the darkness.*

## Round 2

### Dungeon Master (9.4s)

*I halt mid-step, my axe raised but not yet striking. The dragon's words hit me like a physical blow - not the mindless beast I expected, but something far more dangerous. Intelligent. Calculating.*

"Aria..." *I spit the name like a curse, my knuckles white around my weapon.* "So you have a name. Good. I want to know what to carve on your gravestone."

*But something in her tone, her casual dismissal, makes me pause. I keep my shield up, ready to move, but I don't charge.*

"Others? How many have you killed, wyrm? How many villages burned? How many clans scattered to the winds?" *My voice cracks slightly with old grief.* "Ten years ago, a dragon - perhaps you, perhaps your kin - turned Ironhold Keep to rubble. My brothers, my father, my uncle... all of them ash."

*I take another step forward, but slower now, studying her.*

"You speak of patience and dances, but I see treasure behind you. Gold that might have come from Millhaven's coffers. So tell me, *Aria* - why should I believe you're anything more than another greedy beast terrorizing innocents?"

*My grip shifts on the axe, ready to strike or defend.*

"Give me one good reason not to end this here and now."

### Thorin (10.9s)

**[Aria]**: *The dragon's eyes narrow slightly at your accusations, a low rumble emanating from her throat - not quite a growl, but close.*

**"Ironhold Keep..."** *She tilts her massive head, as if searching through memories.* **"Ten years past? That was Vyrakthos the Red, my... predecessor in these mountains. A brutish creature with no subtlety whatsoever."**

*She shifts, her claws clicking against the stone floor, but makes no aggressive moves.*

**"As for Millhaven's 'terrorizing' - I have taken precisely three sheep, two goats, and one very rude merchant who tried to shortchange me on a perfectly reasonable trade agreement. Hardly the wholesale slaughter you describe, little dwarf."**

*Her tail lashes once, betraying some irritation.*

**"The treasure you see? Accumulated over centuries through trade, tribute, and yes - the occasional... taxation of those who pass through my domain. But burned villages? Murdered families? You mistake me for a far cruder sort of dragon."**

*She leans forward slightly, her golden eyes gleaming.*

**"Your grief is real, Thorin Ironforge - yes, I know your name. But perhaps your vengeance is misdirected. Vyrakthos has been dead these past three years, slain by a knight in the eastern kingdoms."**

**"So... what now, dwarf? Will you strike down the wrong dragon out of wounded pride?"**

## Round 3

### Dungeon Master (9.6s)

*My axe wavers, the certainty that has driven me for ten years suddenly cracking like ice in spring. The name hits me like a hammer blow.*

"Vyrakthos..." *I whisper, then louder,* "Vyrakthos the Red. You... you knew him?"

*I don't lower my weapon, but I stop advancing. My breathing is heavy, confused.*

"Dead? Three years dead?" *A bitter laugh escapes me.* "And here I've been sharpening my blade, dreaming of the day I'd bury it in dragon-scale..."

*I study her more carefully now, noting the differences - the size, the coloring, the intelligence in those golden eyes.*

"But the villagers of Millhaven speak of terror, of a dragon's shadow over their homes. If not wholesale slaughter, then what? What 'trade agreements' and 'taxation' do you speak of?"

*My grip on the axe loosens slightly, but I remain ready.*

"And that 'rude merchant' - what did he do that earned him... what fate exactly?" *My voice hardens again.* "Because if you're just a more clever sort of killer, dragon, then perhaps the wrong target is better than no target at all."

*I take a half-step back, reassessing everything.*

"Speak plainly, Aria. What is your game with Millhaven? And why should I believe a word from a creature that admits to 'taxing' travelers?"

### Thorin (10.1s)

**[Aria]**: *The dragon's expression shifts, showing what might almost be amusement - or perhaps exasperation.*

**"The merchant? Ah, Gareth Goldtooth. He promised me fifty gold pieces for safe passage through the mountain pass, then tried to pay with copper coins painted gold. When I confronted him, he had the audacity to draw steel on me."** 

*She gestures with one claw toward a corner of the cavern where armor and weapons lie in a neat pile.*

**"He now serves as my... librarian. Cataloging my collection. Quite alive, though considerably more humble than before."**

*Her tail curls thoughtfully.*

**"As for Millhaven - I offered them a simple arrangement. I clear the mountain passes of bandits and worse creatures, ensure safe trade routes, and in return they provide a modest tribute. A few livestock, some crafted goods, nothing that would starve them."**

*Her eyes flash with irritation.*

**"But their new mayor - a pompous fool named Aldric - declared me a 'menace to be destroyed' and posted bounties instead of honoring the agreement his predecessor made. So yes, I've been... demonstrative. Flying low over the village, roaring at dawn. Reminding them of what they're refusing."**

*She leans back, studying you intently.*

**"Tell me, dwarf - is it truly justice you seek, or simply an enemy to bleed upon your blade?"**
---

## Round 4

### Dungeon Master (10.2s)

*I lower my axe slowly, the weight of it suddenly feeling different in my hands. The rage that's sustained me for a decade wavers, replaced by something more complicated.*

"Justice..." *I repeat the word like I'm tasting it for the first time in years.* "I thought I knew what that meant. Thought it had claws and scales and breathed fire."

*I look around the cavern - at the neat pile of armor, the organized treasure, the signs of... civilization.*

"A librarian? You keep a librarian?" *Despite everything, there's almost a hint of dark amusement in my voice.* "And this arrangement with the previous mayor - was it... fair? Or just extortion with prettier words?"

*I finally lower my shield, though I don't set down my weapons entirely.*

"I've spent ten years hating dragons, Aria. All dragons. It was simpler that way - cleaner. But if Vyrakthos is dead, and you're not the beast that destroyed my clan..." *I shake my head, confused and angry at my own confusion.*

"Then what am I supposed to do with all this rage? What am I supposed to do with ten years of sharpening steel and nursing hatred?"

*I look directly into her golden eyes.*

"And what of Millhaven? If their mayor broke an agreement, that's one thing. But the people shouldn't suffer for his pride. There has to be a better way than terrorizing them with dawn flights."

### Thorin (10.7s)

**[Aria]**: *The dragon settles more comfortably, her posture becoming less threatening as she considers your words.*

**"Fair? Perhaps not by your standards, little dwarf. But consider - before my arrangement, three merchant caravans were lost to mountain bandits in a single season. Wolves and worse things hunted right to Millhaven's gates. I eliminated those threats."**

*She gestures with one claw toward the cavern's depths.*

**"The tribute was modest - far less than they saved in guards and losses. And I even provided... consultation. Weather warnings, news from other settlements, knowledge of safe routes."**

*Her expression grows more thoughtful, almost melancholy.*

**"As for your rage, Thorin Ironforge... I understand it better than you might think. I too have lost kin to violence - other dragons, mostly, competing for territory. The anger becomes... familiar, doesn't it? Like an old cloak you're afraid to remove because you've forgotten what you wore beneath it."**

*She rises slightly, moving toward a section of wall covered in what appear to be maps and documents.*

**"But perhaps your steel could serve a different purpose. Mayor Aldric's pride endangers his people - bandits have already begun testing the passes again. If a respected warrior were to... mediate... perhaps we could reach a new understanding."**

*She looks back at you with calculating eyes.*

**"What say you, dwarf? Channel that decade of preparation toward protecting the innocent rather than pursuing ghosts?"**
