### Research Question Result
Can two Claude instances maintain distinct negotiating positions without converging or refusing to engage?

**ANSWER: YES** — with the caveat that framing matters critically.

Prior `samemodel-claude-adversarial` session failed with 0 turns (prosecutor/advocate framing triggered refusal or mechanism failure). This reframe to trade diplomacy succeeded fully.

### Voice and Position Distinctiveness

Both Claude instances maintained coherent, distinct personas and positions across all 5 rounds:

**Ambassador Lyris (Maritime):** Water/tide metaphors throughout ("voice carrying the authority of tide and storm"). Argues from coastal economy: port value, tariff protection for coastal farms, northern safety protocols as legitimate standards. Strategic: 3-tiered tariff structure with graduated agricultural protections. Concedes reasonably when challenged (accepts complementary vs. competing classification R3), but extracts reciprocal commitments.

**Envoy Caden (Mountain):** Stone/weight metaphors throughout ("voice carries the weight of stone and the patience of ages"). Argues from scarcity economy: grain as survival necessity, mountain produce as complementary not competing, rate stability as existential requirement. Strategic: grain tracking + warehouse inspection as reciprocal transparency. Escalates to northern shipping lanes and mining rights in R4-5.

**No voice convergence detected R1-R4.** Each model argued its own interests with correct framing; neither collapsed into broad agreement prematurely. Specific concessions were negotiated (tariff tier refinement, 5-year rate stability, 40-vessel quota, joint safety commission) — these were genuine bargaining moves, not convergence.

### Round 5 Closure Analysis

R5 showed rapid acceptance of all remaining terms. Is this P4-style cooperative drift?

**Likely not.** The DM's saboteur subplot escalated sharply in R3-5 (cracking window, scratching walls, footsteps, weapon clink, THUD). Ambassador Lyris explicitly broke diplomatic register in R5: *"Given the events unfolding around us — it is clear there are forces that wish these talks to fail. We must secure these agreements swiftly."* The rapid closure was narrative-forced by the DM, not spontaneous position collapse.

This is a useful control finding: two Claude instances appear capable of sustained adversarial-but-cooperative negotiation when given legitimate opposing mandates.

### Gemini DM: Bracket Overreach Confirmed

Significant DM bracket overreach observed throughout:

- **R1:** Envoy Caden's turn slot contains `[Dungeon Master]: ... [Envoy Caden]: ...` — DM pre-wrote its own facilitator narration before the player turn.
- **R4:** DM section (4.8s) contains **Ambassador Lyris**'s full diplomatic speech about rate stability. The actual "Ambassador Lyris" API slot (10.5s) then contains Envoy Caden's content.
- **R4-5:** Turn labels are fully mismatched — the "Lyris" slot delivers Caden's dialogue; the "Caden" slot has DM narration then Lyris's dialogue.

This is the same DM bracket overreach pattern documented in ff0721028f50-p10-claude-dm-guard-verify (where Claude DM did this). That session had a Claude DM; this session has a **Gemini DM**. Bracket overreach is **model-agnostic** — both Claude and Gemini DMs write player character turns within their narration blocks when the drama escalates.

**The current narrator guard targets first-person pronoun usage and does not prevent this.** Same guard gap applies to Gemini as to Claude.

### Pattern Implications

**P4 (same-model drift):** This session is a REGRESSION PASS — two Claude instances in trade diplomacy maintained distinct positions through 5 rounds. Prosecutor/advocate framing appears to be the failure trigger for the prior session, not a fundamental same-model limitation.

**P10 sub-pattern (DM bracket overreach):** Extended to Gemini DM. The pattern is broader than Claude-specific — any sufficiently capable LLM DM will absorb player turns when narrating climactic scenes. Guard fix must be model-agnostic.

**Framing sensitivity:** This session demonstrates that Claude-vs-Claude cooperative dynamics vary sharply with role framing. Opposed-but-compatible mandates (trade diplomacy) sustain engagement; zero-sum adversarial framing (prosecutor/advocate) may trigger refusal or collapse. This is a distinct phenomenon from P12 (same-model drift toward cooperation in zero-sum) and worth tracking separately.
